The power of the spill is a multidisciplinary performance that emerges at the intersection of digital and imaginary technologies. A movement-choreography informed by somatic practices reconfigures the space by projecting back the image of a camera altered by feedback processes created through live coding.. It proposes, that way we experience the world (which experience arises through and across the body) and the categories we create through imagery have social consequences; they shape our value-systems and determine what kinds of thoughts, movements, relationships become possible.

The performance involves a live video recording including selected objects, bodies and the space itself. The trajectory of the recording is a site-specific movement-choreography with the camera, based on somatic techniques informed by Body-Mind-Centering and Continuum Movement. The footage is projected back into the same space in an altered form, gradually inhabited by improvised generative processes created through live coding. As this modified image takes over the raw recording, it opens up a visual of life where edges are multiple, selves always leak, spill over, are impossible to contain.

The power of the spill is a study on visual perception and how it affects our ways of making sense of the world, aiming to create an alternative lens that acknowledges the vitality of objects, a topology that is cross-species, the ways seemingly separate entities are in constant exchange, towards a more ecological way of being.
